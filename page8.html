Maldives Surf Spots are some of the best waves in the world, with endless Surf Breaks scattered among the countries Coral Islands. The North Male' Atoll is the best region to Surf in Maldives. The Swell size averages from shoulder to head high to double over head, making for an awesome beginner, intermediate and also advanced Surf Trip to Maldives.

Jailbreaks

Situated on this Island, you will find a perfect right wave fairly fast that starts off low and growing as it advances. The name is because the break is just in front of the Prison in the Island. This break is probably the fastest wave in the Maldives with long walls and 3 tube sections. The best swell is South West with wind from South East. You can reach this surf poin by walk as its located in this Island. You can also take a boat which will take 08 minutes to reach there. The cost of the boat is $10 per person.

Sultans

Located in the Island Thanburudhoo, a very fast right hand wave that works better with bigger swell and can become very long with swell from the south, best conditions are found with winds from the west and high tide. If Honky’s is onshore, then Sutans will be offshore. It is one of the most consistent waves and there will always be something to surf. You can reach this point by a boat within 08 minutes and it costs $10 per person for the boat.

MORE

Hokys

Located on the same island as Sultans (Thanburudhoo), some consider it the best wave of the Maldives. When the conditions are met, it is a true World Class, that’s for sure! It is a super long, left-hand wave that can double in the second section. It is a fast left at 4-6ft and needs the right swell direction. Worth checking out if sultans is crowded. It is an ideal spot for winter on North-East wind. You can reach this point by a boat which will take 08 minutes to reach. And it costs $10 per person for the boat.

MORE

Ninjas

Slow right-hander, good for beginners and long-borders. Presumably named because it’s one of the favorite waves of the Japanese surfers. Best conditions on W-NW winds with a moderate South Swell. However swells larger than 4ft ruin it. You have to take a boat to reach this point. It takes 12 minutes to reach here by boat and costs $20 per person for the boat. 

MORE

Cokes

Located in the Island Thulusdhoo. This is a right-handed wave recommended for intermediate surfers or experienced surfers when the swell is big. The wave it is a hollow and tubular right-hander that breaks over a shallow reef. The wave’s name is due to the Coca-Cola factory operating on the Island Thulusdhoo. Works best with winds from South and North-East. You have to take a boat to reach this point. It takes 30 minutes to reach here by boat and costs $100 total for the boat for maximum 06 persons.

MORE

Chickens

Wave located in an uninhabited Island, next to Thulusdhoo Island (on the other side of the channel). This wave holds swell size from 3ft to well over 10ft and provides rides up to 500m long. This is a long left-hand wave with 2 sections where you can easily get barrels on the last section of the wave. It will take 30 minutes to reach this point by boat and costs $100 total for the boat trip.

MORE

Furana (Tombstones)

Located nearby the Sheraton Full Moon resort. The first break that you see when you’re coming from Airport to this Island. The rights are fairly fickle, until a 2m + SE swell hits with a 12 sec + period, transforming it into one of the best rides. Can be reached by boat which would take 15 minutes to reach there. It costs $50 for the boat trip.
